Inverter installation:


An inverter is necessary to convert the DC electricity produced by the solar panels into AC electricity that can be used by home appliances.

Inverters are available in different types and sizes. The type of inverter you need will depend on the specific requirements of your solar panel system. Some factors to consider when choosing an inverter include:

  • Input voltage range: The input voltage range of an inverter must be compatible with the output voltage of the solar panels.
  • Output power: The output power of an inverter must be sufficient to meet the power needs of the appliances you plan to use.
  • Efficiency: Inverters convert DC electricity into AC electricity with some loss of energy. More efficient inverters will have lower losses and thus be more effective at powering appliances.
  • Size: Inverters come in different sizes, so you will need to choose one that is appropriately sized for your needs.
  • Features: Inverters can have different features, such as built-in battery chargers, USB ports, and remote control capabilities. Choose an inverter with the features you need.
